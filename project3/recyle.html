Healthy Eating

How to develop a healthy eating pattern

You can develop a healthy eating pattern by:

  1. Eating more vegetables and fruits
  2. dark green such as broccoli and bok choy
  3. orange such as carrots
  4. Selecting whole grain varieties of grain foods
  5. whole grain pasta
  6. whole grain bread
  7. whole oats or oatmeal
  8. whole grain brown or wild rice
  9. Replacing meats higher in saturated fat with leaner varieties
  10. Replacing some of the meat in your meals with plant-based protein foods
  11. Limiting highly processed foods

Limit highly processed foods

Highly processed foods are processed or prepared foods and drinks that add excess sodium, sugars or saturated fat to the diets of Canadians.

  • How to limit highly processed foods
    • replace sugary drinks with water
    • choose healthier menu options when eating out
    • make homemade versions of your favourite highly processed foods
  • Highly Processed Foods include
    • sugary drinks
    • syrups and jams
    • chocolate and candies
    • potato chips and pretzels
    • sauces, dressings and gravies
    • ice cream and frozen desserts
    • fast foods like French fries and burgers

Make Water your drink of choice

You can add fruits and herbs to your hot or cold water for flavour.

  • 5 ways to add flavour to your water
    • blackberries + mint
    • raspberries + cucumber
    • strawberries + fresh basil
    • chopped apples + a cinnamon stick
    • pear slices + a drop of vanilla extract
  • If you like some fizz in your drink, try carbonated water.
  • Choose drinks that have little to no added sodium, sugars or saturated fat.
